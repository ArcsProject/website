The Center of Excellence ROMANIAN IN ACTION offers innovative classes and workshops for children and youth of Romanian origin and background. Regardless of your child’s knowledge of Romanian, we will use total or partial immersion language pedagogies and adjust our instructional strategies to her/his particular learning needs and potential. Our programs plant the seeds for life success for Romanian heritage students by nurturing creative enrichment, academic excellence and appreciation for bilingual education.

What?

The project-base workshops are as follows:

Creative Workshops (5-7 years)

Instructor: Elizabeta Iliescu

This class starts from the premise that every single child is a potential artist! Building on the innate talent of each student, creative workshops will use painting, modeling, collages, fables and personal stories to develop communication skills in Romanian. The students will learn how to describe and present the heroes of various Romanian stories in view of creating their own characters and scenarios. Romanian poems, songs, and games will be used to develop the children’s cultural sensitivity, enrich their vocabulary and nurture appreciation for their heritage language.

Around the World (8-13 years)

Instructor: Otilia Baraboi

Through the use of authentic, interactive contexts and techniques, the students will learn more about Romania and world geography, nature, environment and cultures. They will produce artifacts depicting each step of their journey around the world and will compile a portfolio of postcards, letters, diary entries, photo-albums, maps, etc. Romanian language and communication skills will be acquired naturally, through interactive activities, and in a structured format through age-appropriate texts that will include vocabulary, idiomatic expressions, and useful grammatical aspects. Sensorial learning, such as music, dance, and even food will enrich their discovery adventure.

The Secret Society of Romanian Story Tellers, First Workshop (12-17 years)

Instructor: Adriana-Guiman

The Secret Society of Romanian Storytellers is a creative environment where participants are encouraged to explore elements from their Romanian identity through storytelling, a space where they can build friendships, and the confidence to keep discovering their Romanian story. Stories of Romanian objects, the story of their family or the story of an imaginary visit in Romania can be shaped in a multi-platform storytelling experience using immersive technologies such as virtual reality, and augmented reality.

Read more...

This first workshop is two sessions learning experience (with a week pause between them) that will teach the participants the basic skills for developing a multi-platform storytelling experience.
In the first session the participants will get hands on how to prototype a cardboard game, how to produce audio and video content, 360° video, and will learn the basic steps for creating a VR and AR experience.
They will learn how to approach a story from a multi-platform perspective, and why this approach is important. They will experience 360°, VR and AR content that will serve as models for creating their own projects prototypes.
In the break between the session’s participants will come up with ideas for their own projects and receive support for partially producing them, through online tutoring.
The second session of the workshop will be built around their own projects, working on editing and combining all the elements into a final multi-platform project production plan. We will work towards having at least two prototypes produced/per project at the end of the two sessions.

Instructors :


Ileana Marin

ileana.marin@arcsproject.org

Ileana Marin graduated Faculty of Letters at the Uniiversity of Bucharest in 1991. She began her academic career at the Ovidius University of Constanța where she taught comparative literature, coordinated International Summer Courses of Romanian Language, Culture, and Civilization for which she also taught Romanian between 1992 and 2004. In 2000, she earned her Ph.D. in philology from the University of Bucharest and later became an associate professor. From 2006, she has taught comparative literature courses at the University of Washington where she defended her second Ph.D. in textual studies in 2011. Since 2010, she has collaborated with the Center of Excellence in Image Studies – University of Bucharest where she coordinates doctoral candidates. Ileana Marin taught Romanian as a Fulbright scholar in 2004 and as an instructor for FLAS fellows in 2007 at the UW. In 2016, together with Otilia Baraboi, Gloria Man, and Iulian Calinov, she participated in launching the pilot program "Româna la Liceu".

Otilia Baraboi

otilia.baraboi@arcsproject.org

Otilia Baraboi is a higher education professional who has been teaching French language and literature at the University of Washington for the past 17 years. She emigrated to the US in 2000 after having obtained a BA with a double specialization in French and English languages and literatures from the Al.I. Cuza University in Iasi. She also holds a Master’s degree (DEA) from the University of Geneva and a Ph.D. in French Literature from the University of Washington. Before coming to the US, Otilia worked as a literary journalist, published short-fiction and translations, and was actively involved in the cultural life of her hometown, Iasi. For Otilia, becoming part of the pioneer projects "Româna la Liceu" and the Center of Excellence ROMANIAN IN ACTION represents a long-awaited opportunity to invest in the future of the Romanian American community and, on a more personal level, to have a positive impact on the ways in which her own two boys define and pass on their rich cultural and linguistic heritage.

Elizabeta Iliescu

elizabeta.iliescu@arcsproject.org

Elizabeta Iliescu was born in Northern Romania where she grew up very attached to her grandparents. After high school, she moved to Bucharest where she studied physics and worked for an insurance company. She moved to the US with her family in 2008. The love of being around children made her look for opportunities to work with them After earning an AAS-T Certificate in early Childhood Careers, she volunteered and worked in various early childhood education programs. Elizabeta is currently working with Bellevue School District in Early Learning Programs and volunteering for the ARCS Piticot program. She is also collaborating with Lakehills Library for the program Romanian Story Times / Poveşti româneşti.

Adriana Guiman

adriana.guiman@arcsproject.org

Adriana Guiman’s background is in film and media production. She has a Master Degree in Transmedia Storytelling Production from Tallinn & Tartu University, Estonia, and is currently researching cinematic VR experiences as a PhD student at Aalto University, Helsinki, Finland. She is collaborating with the company Engage Through Tech from Bellevue, facilitating media and tech workshops for the Girl Scouts of Washington. She is the founder of the company XR Journeys that focuses on storytelling through immersive technologies.
