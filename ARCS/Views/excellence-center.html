THE CENTER OF EXCELLENCE

Are you interested in stimulating your child’s creativity by nourishing her/his bicultural and bilingual background?

Registration

The Center of Excellence ROMANIAN IN ACTION offers innovative programs for children and youth of Romanian origin and background. Following the most recent trends in education, our experiential approaches develop intellectual and imaginative skills while creating fun, student-driven learning environments tailored to each level of language competence. Regardless of your child’s knowledge of Romanian, we will use total or partial immersion language pedagogies and adjust our instructional strategies to her/his particular learning needs and potential.

Theories by Wittgenstein, Bialystok, and de Bruin show that bilingual education is an essential aspect of building and developing beautiful minds, of achieving mental flexibility, exercising memory and attention, as well as of developing exceptional cognitive abilities. Numerous articles and extensive statistics emphasize the advantages that bilingual students have over their monolingual peers.

Our programs ultimately plant the seeds for life success for Romanian heritage students by nurturing creative enrichment and academic excellence through bilingual and immersion education.

The programs designed for the Center of Excellence ROMANIAN IN ACTION use the Romanian language as a catalyst of the process of cognition and the assimilation of Romanian cultural values and traditions. In the context of contemporary American culture, students will have the opportunity to experiment with and relate to the heritage of their parents and grandparents, and to develop relations and friendships with other students with whom they share a common heritage. Creating a sense of belonging to the American Romanian community and contributing to a solid personal identity are among the major objectives of our programs.

How?

All programs of the Center of Excellence in Romanian are interactive and interdisciplinary. The programs are focused on stimulating creativity, divergent knowledge (the way of navigating several knowledge levels), and the practice of linguistic structures appropriate to the students’ age and language competence in simulating real life contexts.

Each class-activity will have 3 crucial parts:

  1. The part in which Romanian language knowledge and skills are passed to children in a structured format through texts appropriate to the students’ age, texts that will include vocabulary, idiomatic expressions, and grammar structures which will become the basis for the students’ texts, starting from simple written genres (note, short message, list) to complex ones (essay, article, composition) in accordance with their competence and grouping;
  2. The creative project part that contributes to the assimilation of linguistic knowledge that has been delivered in a structured format.
  3. The socio-behavioral part which facilitates group relations based on the same cultural heritage.

What?

The project-base workshops are as follows:

Puppet Theatre / Shadow Theatre will be offered to the Elves and Warriors groups as they are very attractive / engaging ways to place the children in natural contexts for their ages to learn and converse in Romanian through simulating intellectual, artistic, and technical creativity. During the Puppet Theatre workshop, Elves and Warriors will create the characters and the plot,as well as build the props necessary to the show that will be performed publicly at the end of the semester.

Read more...

Poems, songs, and Romanian games will be used to develop the children’s artistic sensitivity, to explain and drill the vocabulary necessary for the staging of the play. The goals of the Puppet Theatre workshop are developing communication skills in Romanian, enriching the vocabulary, developing manual dexterity as a stage prior to writing (cut, glue, paint, draw), and acquiring team-work skills.

Creative Workshops will start from the premise that every single child is a potential artist! Building on the innate talent of each Elf and Warrior, creative workshops will use painting, modeling, collage, fables and personal stories to access the inner world of each child in order to develop communication skills in Romanian. Visual representation will give children opportunities to know themselves and to know others, as well as to communicate about themselves and their surrounding reality.

Read more...

The goals of creative workshops are to practice group interaction (Elves and Warriors will learn how to describe, characterize, and present the heroes of their stories from a physical and emotional point of view, emphasizing their heroes’ positive and negative features, understanding their actions, etc.), to be able to tell short stories (they will formulate sentences about their heroes thus practicing structures of Romanian, direct and indirect styles and organizing the discourse coherently and logically), and to develop the cognitive function of the language.

Drama is a personal growth workshop dedicated to the Giants group. Starting from the classical texts of Romanian literature, the Giants will create own their plots and heroes, and will stage their adaptations, reflecting their own life experience. They will learn to read, write, communicate in Romanian while performing the heroes they created in a public show at the end of the semester.

Read more...

Ballads, stories, and fairytales from Romanian literature will be adapted to contemporary life.

The goals of the drama workshop are developing coherent and structured communication skills both written and oral, utilizing an adequate body language, articulating sound opinions as the result of an analytical evaluation that proves the clear understanding of messages. Another major objective of the drama workshop is for kids to get acquainted with conversation strategies in order to be able to formulate either a different point of view or consensus.

Live Reporting is a workshop for the Giants group and proposes to develop their attention, observation, creative thinking and nuanced communication in Romanian. By engaging role playing and communication skills of reporting, Giants will choose and document themes of personal interest (everyday life, nature, friends from animal realm, sports, and food) and will be trained to formulate specific questions in order to receive meaningful and complex answers.

Read more...

Ballads, stories, and fairytales from Romanian literature will be adapted to contemporary life.

The goals of the Live Reporting workshop are the enlarging of Romanian vocabulary, the elaborating and communicating information on a specific theme. Additionally, this program focuses on the social skills, ways of interaction with peers, encouraging empathy, and assertive communication.

Around the World proposes a virtual journey aiming to cultivate the Explorers’ interest in nature, geography, environment, cultural diversity, and human races. The Explorers will get familiar with old and new technologies, the means of transportation that may take them through their journey. Through the workshop activities, students will enrich their vocabulary and develop communication skills. They will produce artifacts depicting each step of their journey around the world and will compile a portfolio with postcards, letters, diary entries, photo-albums, maps, etc. Thus, students will use Romanian in authentic and interactive contexts in which they will engage body language and sensorial activities such as music, dance and, even food.

Read more...

The goals of this workshop are the fostering of a global awareness, eagerness to learn about history, science, nature, and the cultures around the world, thus offering the Explorers the tools to understand the complexity of our world while speaking Romanian.

Dramatization is designed as an interdisciplinary and interactive activity for the Bravehearts and Explorers groups. It combines history and visual art with role playing and improvisation. The roles are inspired by historical paintings of Romanian classics. Students will retrace the lives of the heroes up to the moment of their pictorial representation. Staging narrative paintings which present events from Romanian culture, the students will learn and practice the art of improvisation, the first step toward fluent and free communication while using complex syntax.

Read more...

The goals of this workshop are to facilitate knowledge and understanding of Romanian historical and cultural events, exploration of the Romanian artistic heritage, analysis of artistic strategies and nuanced expression in Romanian while using all three tenses: past, present, and future.

Community Voices initiates the dialog between generations that will lead to the archiving of private histories and life experiences of members of the Romanian community in Washington State. The Bravehearts will establish relations with the seniors of the local Romanian community and with successful professionals from several areas. They will receive both the technical and linguistic training that will be necessary to record and transcribe identity stories that will compensate the lack of extensive families in the US. Students will thus gain a cultural sensitivity and a feeling of belonging to their family’s heritage that will be facilitated by a close knowledge and interaction with our community members with whom Bravehearts will meet regularly.

Read more...

The goals of this workshop are the gaining of knowledge and the passing of the collective memory of the Romanian community in the Seattle larger area onto the second generation, as well as the making more visible Romanian-American role models of successful professionals.

ARCS team will gather the stories and interviews recorded by the Bravehearts into a volume that will be published online and / or in print with the support of prestigious institutions such as 4Culture (www.4culture.org), Jack Straw Cultural Center (www.jackstraw.org), and Ethnic Cultural Council (www.ethnicheritagecouncil.org).

COMPETENCES IN ROMANIAN / SOCIO-CULTURAL COMPENTENCES
AGE GROUP
3-5 years Group Elves

At the end of the year, kids will be able to

  • to understand isolated words and simple expressions
  • memorize and reproduce short poems and songs
  • express basic necessities
  • respect the rules of communication in a group
5-7 years Group Warriors

At the end of the year, kids will be able to

  • express courtesy
  • understand simple sentences
  • formulate messages about surrounding reality, using present tense
  • respect the rules of communication in a group
  • make friends with the peers
7-10 years Group Giants

At the end of the year, kids will be able to

  • participate in basic conversations
  • formulate messages using present and future tense
  • formulate plans for the near future
  • work in a team
  • develop empathy
  • develop a better appreciation of the Romanian cultural heritage
10-14 years Group Bravehearts

At the end of the year, kids will be able to

  • discuss themes of general interest
  • narrate using past tense
  • demonstrate persuasion by using adequate strategies
  • gain cultural sensitivity and the feeling of belonging to the Romanian cultural heritage
14-18 years Group Explorers

At the end of the year, kids will be able to

  • understand and use a rich vocabulary
  • understand and use idiomatic expressions
  • use the conditional tense
  • discuss possible situations
  • develop strategies of assertive communication
  • value bicultural and bilingual literacy as global citizens
14-18 years (advanced) Romanian in HS

At the end of the year, kids will be able to

  • understand and use proverbs
  • discuss scientific topics
  • use complex grammar structures
  • develop strategies of assertive communication
  • understand the concept of socio-cultural identity

Ileana Marin

Ileana Marin graduated Faculty of Letters at the Uniiversity of Bucharest in 1991. She began her academic career at the Ovidius University of Constanța where she taught comparative literature, coordinated International Summer Courses of Romanian Language, Culture, and Civilization for which she also taught Romanian between 1992 and 2004. Otilia Baraboi
